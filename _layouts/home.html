Abstract

UCL MotionInput is a touchless computing platform developed by UCL students over the last 3 years. It is currently on its third major revision and features hand, body, head, eyes and speech modules. These allow users to control a Windows machine and play games using these body parts simply using a webcam. However, it lacked functionality for the lower body, and that area is the focus of our project, which will be a key part of MotionInput v3.2. Our goal is to make MotionInput more accessible to those with more limited upper body movement, thus allowing them to have more independence.
We were tasked with designing a virtual dance mat, where users can trigger designated inputs by stepping forward, left, right and backwards, while in view of the camera. This can be used to play more basic video games that require fewer inputs. It can also be combined with other interactions such as hands and speech in order to do more complex tasks. We have also developed additional actions – for example recognising a “kick” gesture for football games such as FIFA, as well as a jitter smoothing algorithm for the hands module of MotionInput that would allow users with hand tremors to make mouse usage easier.
